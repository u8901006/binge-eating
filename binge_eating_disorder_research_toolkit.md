# Binge Eating Disorder Research Toolkit

**Scope:** medical, neuroscience, psychology, sociology, nutrition, and exercise/sports medicine journals; topic positioning; common keywords; and reusable search templates for PubMed and other academic search engines.

**Use case:** rapid literature mapping, systematic review scoping, clinical update searches, SEO/education research, thesis topic discovery, and citation harvesting.

**Coverage note:** No list can literally include every journal that might occasionally publish on binge eating disorder (BED). This toolkit prioritizes journals that are repeatedly relevant to BED, eating disorders, obesity, appetite, psychiatry, psychology, neuroscience, nutrition, public health, sociology, and physical activity/sports medicine.

## 12. Quality-Control Checklist

Before saving results into a reference manager:

- Confirm whether `BED` means **binge eating disorder**, not another abbreviation.
- Separate **binge eating behavior** from DSM-defined **binge eating disorder**.
- Check whether the sample is clinical, community, bariatric, obesity-treatment, adolescent, athlete, or college-based.
- Note whether diagnosis used structured interview, EDE/EDE-Q, self-report, screening tool, or chart diagnosis.
- Separate BED from bulimia nervosa when compensatory behaviors are present.
- Record sex/gender distribution; men are often underrepresented in eating disorder research.
- Check whether weight-loss trials excluded active eating disorders.
- Avoid equating BED with “food addiction” unless the paper explicitly tests that framework.
- For nutrition/exercise studies, check whether the intervention might unintentionally increase restraint, shame, or compensatory behavior.
- For social media studies, distinguish cross-sectional association from causal evidence.
- For pharmacotherapy studies, record adverse effects, psychiatric comorbidity, and relapse after discontinuation.
- For bariatric studies, distinguish preoperative BED, postoperative loss-of-control eating, grazing, and weight regain.
